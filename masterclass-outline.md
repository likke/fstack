## PART 1: THE CONTENT OPS FRAMEWORK (0:10–0:40)

**Transition:**
> "Let me start with the most important shift you need to make. It's not about using more AI tools. It's about understanding where in the content process your value actually lives."

### The 4-Layer Stack

Show visually — four layers stacked:

```
[ GOVERNANCE ]  ← Where you become unfireable
[ AI TOOLS   ]  ← Where most people stop
[ PRODUCTION ]  ← What AI commoditized
[ STRATEGY   ]  ← Where you become unfireable
```

**Layer 1 — Strategy**
- "This is the 'why' layer. Without this, all your AI outputs are just noise."
- "AI can suggest topics. It cannot decide what your brand stands for."
- "Most marketers think strategy is the brief. It's not. The brief is production. Strategy is what comes before the brief."

**Layer 2 — Production**
- "This is where most marketers spend 80% of their time. This is also the layer AI hit hardest."
- "Writing a blog post used to take 4 hours. AI can draft one in 4 minutes. If your entire value is in the drafting — you're in trouble."
- "AI drafts are 60–70% of the way there. The gap between 70% and publishable? That's still human."

**Layer 3 — AI Tools**
- "The tool doesn't matter as much as the system around it."
- "90% of marketers are using AI like a search engine — they type, they accept, they publish. That's how you get off-brand, generic content."
- "The marketers who stay use AI like a junior team member — you brief it, you review it, you approve or reject."
- Stat: 30–40% of AI outputs contain errors or hallucinations if left unchecked. That's a quality problem. Quality problems are a governance problem.

**Layer 4 — Governance**
- "This is the layer nobody talks about. And it's the most valuable one."
- "Governance is what separates a content operation from content chaos."
- "When you have governance, you can scale. You can hand work to AI, to freelancers, to junior team members — and the output stays on brand."
- Stat: Fewer than 30% of organizations using generative AI for content have formal governance policies. (Gartner, 2025) That gap is your opportunity — and your clients' pain point.

**The punchline:**
> "Most marketers only live in Layer 2. AI commoditized Layer 2. The unfireable marketer works at Layers 1 and 4 — strategy and governance. That's what we're building today."

## PART 2: HOW TO BRIEF, DIRECT & REVIEW AI WORK (0:45–1:10)

**Transition:**
> "Now I want to get practical. Because the #1 mistake I see marketers make with AI is they skip the brief. They think the more detailed their prompt, the better the output. That's partly true — but a prompt is not a brief. Let me show you the difference."

### Why Most AI Outputs Are Mediocre

- "You give AI a vague request → you get a vague output. Garbage in, garbage out."
- "But the real problem isn't the prompt. It's the missing context. AI doesn't know your brand voice. It doesn't know your clients. It doesn't know your standards."
- "That's your job. And when you structure that knowledge into a brief — the output changes completely."

### The 3-Part AI Brief Framework

**Part 1 — CONTEXT**
```
Brand: [Name]
Audience: [Who they are, what they care about, their pain]
Goal: [What this piece of content needs to accomplish]
Tone: [How we talk — 3 adjectives max]
What we never say: [List of off-brand phrases/approaches]
```

**Live example:**
> "Without context: 'Write a LinkedIn post about content marketing for agencies.'
> With context: 'Brand is DashoContent — a content operations platform for agencies. Audience is agency owners frustrated with client content chaos. Goal is to make them feel understood and curious about a better system. Tone is direct, dry, no fluff. We never use the words unlock, leverage, or scale your impact.'"

**Part 2 — CONSTRAINTS**
```
Format: [Post / Email / Blog / Caption]
Length: [Word count or character limit]
Structure: [Any specific headers, sections, CTAs required]
Avoid: [Topics, phrases, formats that don't work for this brand]
```

**Part 3 — STANDARD**
```
This is a good example of our voice: [paste example]
The output should pass this check:
- Sounds like us (not generic AI)
- Has one clear CTA
- No AI tells (filler openers, passive voice, vague claims)
- A client would not be embarrassed if this shipped
```

### The Review Checklist

After every AI output, run this:

- [ ] Does it sound like us? (Read it out loud — if you'd cringe, it fails)
- [ ] Is the CTA right? (One CTA, in the right place)
- [ ] Any AI tells? ("In today's fast-paced world…" / filler phrases / passive voice / vague claims)
- [ ] Any factual claims that need verification?
- [ ] Would a client be embarrassed if this shipped under their name?
