# Brief Review — AI Content Quality Gate
*The skill that makes you unfireable. Built on the same review discipline as Garry Tan's gstack `/review` — applied to content operations.*

> *"That's what makes you unfireable — you're not doing the tasks, you're running the operation."*

AI doesn't replace the marketer. It changes what level they need to work at. The ones who stay are the ones who can **brief AI correctly** and **review its output ruthlessly**. This skill teaches both.

---

## Two Modes

### MODE 1: Brief Review (before production)
*Use this BEFORE you give AI a task. Catches vague, incomplete, or wrong briefs before you waste a production cycle.*

**The 5 Brief Quality Checks:**

1. **Audience clarity** — Does the brief name a specific person, not a demographic?
   - Bad: "For marketers in the Philippines"
   - Good: "For a Marketing Manager at a 50-person B2B SaaS company who has never used AI tools before"

2. **Outcome clarity** — Does the brief say what the content needs to DO, not just what it needs to BE?
   - Bad: "Write a LinkedIn post about our new feature"
   - Good: "Write a LinkedIn post that gets Marketing Managers to book a demo — the hook should be about the pain of inconsistent content quality"

3. **Voice clarity** — Does the brief include a tone reference or brand example?
   - Bad: "Sound professional but approachable"
   - Good: "Match the tone of [paste real example]. Short sentences. No corporate jargon. Specific > clever."

4. **Constraint clarity** — Does the brief set limits on format, length, and what NOT to include?
   - Bad: (no constraints)
   - Good: "Max 150 words. No hashtags. No emojis. Don't mention the product name."

5. **Success criteria** — Does the brief say what a good output looks like?
   - Bad: (none)
   - Good: "The first line should make a Marketing Manager stop scrolling. No fluff. Every sentence must earn its place."

**Brief Review Output:**
```
BRIEF SCORE: Strong / Needs work / Too vague to produce good output

WHAT'S MISSING:
- [specific gap]
- [specific gap]

REWRITTEN BRIEF:
[improved version with all 5 checks met]

EXPECTED OUTPUT QUALITY WITH ORIGINAL BRIEF: Low / Medium / High
EXPECTED OUTPUT QUALITY WITH REWRITTEN BRIEF: High
```

### MODE 2: Output Review (after production)
*Use this AFTER AI produces content. Catches generic output, off-brand language, and "AI slop" before it reaches your client or audience.*

**The 5 Output Quality Checks:**

1. **The Scroll Test** — Would your target reader stop scrolling at the first line?
   - AI default: starts with a generic hook ("In today's digital landscape...")
   - Human standard: starts with a specific, uncomfortable truth or a question that stings

2. **The Brand Voice Test** — Does it sound like the brand, or does it sound like a language model?
   - Red flags: "leverage," "unlock," "empower," "game-changer," "in today's fast-paced world"
   - Green flags: specific, concrete, uses the brand's actual vocabulary

3. **The Specificity Test** — Does it contain real numbers, real names, real examples?
   - AI default: "many companies are seeing results"
   - Human standard: "Barkota shipped 30+ assets last month with zero revision rounds"

4. **The CTA Test** — Is there one clear next action? Only one?
   - AI default: "Like, share, and follow for more content!"
   - Human standard: one action, stated simply, no desperation

5. **The Client Test** — Would your best client be proud to publish this?
   - If you'd hesitate → rewrite it.
   - If it could have been written by anyone → rewrite it.
   - If it passes → ship it.

**Output Review Format:**
```
CONTENT REVIEWED: [describe — LinkedIn post / blog / email / caption / etc.]

SCROLL TEST: Pass / Fail — [first line quoted]
BRAND VOICE TEST: Pass / Fail — [flagged phrases if fail]
SPECIFICITY TEST: Pass / Fail — [what's vague?]
CTA TEST: Pass / Fail — [what's the CTA?]
CLIENT TEST: Would publish proudly / Would hesitate / Would not publish

AI SLOP DETECTED: Yes / No
- [list any generic phrases, filler sentences, or hedging language]

VERDICT: Ship / Rewrite / Brief again
REQUIRED CHANGES: [specific — not vague]

REWRITTEN VERSION (if needed):
[improved version]
```

## The One Rule That Changes Everything

> **If it could have been written by anyone, rewrite it.**

Generic AI output is invisible. It doesn't represent your brand — it represents the average of everything the model was trained on. Your job is not to prompt AI to write. Your job is to make AI write *like you*.

That's the skill that makes you unfireable.

## Quick Reference (Starter Kit Cheat Sheet)

**Before you brief AI, ask:**
- Who exactly is this for?
- What do I need them to DO after reading this?
- What does it need to sound like?
- What are the limits?
- What does a great output look like?

**After AI produces output, ask:**
- Would my target reader stop scrolling at line 1?
- Does this sound like us, or does it sound like a language model?
- Is there anything specific, or is it all general?
- Is there one clear CTA?
- Would I be proud to put my name on this?

**Red flag phrases to always delete:**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Leverage" / "Unlock" / "Empower"
- "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary" / "Cutting-edge"
- "It's no secret that..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Take your [X] to the next level"
