# L10 — Monday Clarity Check
*Built on EOS (Traction by Gino Wickman) + Garry Tan's gstack operating philosophy.*

> *"The L10 meeting is the heartbeat of the business. If it's not happening, the business is not healthy."*
> — Gino Wickman, Traction

> *"The retro is not a performance review. It's a navigation instrument."*
> — fstack `/retro`

EOS gives companies a weekly pulse check — 90 minutes, same agenda every week, designed to be rated a "10 out of 10" by participants every time. Garry Tan's gstack gives engineers a way to make invisible work visible.

This skill merges both: a lightweight, founder-adapted L10 that works for a team of 2-5 people who are moving too fast to do the full EOS process but need the same clarity and traction.

---

## The Six EOS Components (How They Map to fstack)

EOS runs on six components. Every section of the L10 touches one of them:

| EOS Component | What It Is | fstack Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| **Vision** | Where are we going? Is everyone aligned? | Quarterly Rocks check |
| **People** | Right people in right seats? | Team health check |
| **Data** | What do the numbers say? | Scorecard |
| **Issues** | What's in the way? | Issues list (IDS) |
| **Process** | Are we following the system? | Delivery health |
| **Traction** | Are we executing on what matters? | Rocks + To-Dos |

## The L10 Agenda (adapted for solo founders + small teams)

**Total time: 60-90 minutes. Same every Monday.**

### 1. Segue (5 min)
One good news — personal or professional. Forces humans to be human first.
> Ask: "What's one good thing from last week, personal or business?"

### 2. Scorecard (5 min)
Review 5-7 weekly numbers. Are they on track? Red/Yellow/Green.

**Suggested metrics for content ops:**
- MRR (target vs actual)
- Active clients on Barkota model
- On-time delivery rate (target: ≥95%)
- Average revision count (target: ≤2)
- Discovery calls booked this week
- Open client issues / escalations

*If a number is red → drop it to the Issues list. Don't solve it here.*

### 3. Rock Review (5 min)
Quarterly Rocks are your 3-5 biggest priorities for the quarter. Each Rock is either On Track or Off Track. No discussion — just status.

*EOS principle: Rocks are the big stones. If you don't put them in the jar first, the sand fills it up.*

**Example Rocks for a content ops founder:**
- Close 1 new Barkota-model account
- Migrate lowest-margin client to Barkota delivery model
- Cancel tool subscriptions bleeding monthly burn
- Ship brand compliance scoring tool MVP

### 4. Customer/Employee Headlines (5 min)
One-line updates only. Good news or bad news — no solving.
- "Barkota loves the new visibility tracker."
- "OceanJet asked for a custom workflow again."
- "April is overloaded."
- Drop anything negative to the Issues list.

### 5. To-Do List Review (5 min)
Last week's To-Dos: done or not done? 7-day To-Dos only. No 30-day items.
- 90% completion rate is the EOS standard.
- If a To-Do keeps moving week to week → it's either a Rock (too big) or it needs to be killed.

### 6. IDS — Identify, Discuss, Solve (30-60 min)
**This is the most important part.**

The Issues List is where everything that's in the way gets handled. Not vented — *solved.*

EOS's IDS framework:
- **Identify:** State the real issue, not the symptom. (Not "we're losing money on OceanJet" — "OceanJet's revision count is 6x the standard because we have no brief process for them.")
- **Discuss:** Get all perspectives on the table. One round. Not a debate.
- **Solve:** Make a decision and assign a To-Do with an owner and due date. Done.

*Garry Tan's equivalent: gstack `/review` — AUTO-FIXED or ASK. Every issue gets resolved or explicitly deferred. Nothing floats.*

## The EOS Principle That Changes Everything

> *"Rocks, not sand."*

Most teams fill their week with sand — urgent emails, small requests, reactive tasks. EOS forces you to put the big stones (Rocks) in first. Every task that isn't advancing a Rock this week is sand.

Before assigning any To-Do, ask: **"Which Rock does this advance?"**

If the answer is none → it's sand. It waits.

This maps directly to fstack's Revenue Gate:
- A Rock that closes a client → A bucket
- A Rock that retains a client → B bucket
- A Rock that cuts burn → C bucket
- Anything else → sand. Doesn't get a To-Do.

---

## Hard Rules (EOS + fstack)

- Same time, same day, every week. Never cancel.
- No phones. No laptops unless presenting data.
- Issues go on the list — they don't get solved in the Headlines section.
- Every IDS session ends with a To-Do, an owner, and a due date. No exceptions.
- If a Rock has been Off Track for 2 weeks → it gets discussed in IDS immediately.
- If the meeting rating is below 8 → the facilitator asks what would have made it a 10. Fix that next week.
