# Blueprint: Email — From Brand Context to a Sequence That Converts
*A starter kit for marketers using the fstack Brand Identity Layer.*

---

## What this blueprint is

This is a step-by-step workflow for producing a single cold outreach email, nurture email, or multi-email sequence that passes brand-check before it sends. The principles are the same whether you're writing one email or ten.

**Time to complete (first run):** 2–3 hours (most of this is building your brand docs — you only do it once)
**Time to complete (subsequent runs):** 20–45 minutes per email or sequence

## The Workflow

### Step 0 — Load Your Brand Context

Open a new ChatGPT or Claude session. Paste this at the top:

```
I'm producing email content for [BRAND NAME]. Here is my brand context:

VOICE CARD:
[paste your full Voice Card here]

RULES CARD:
[paste your full Rules Card here]

MEMORY — LIVE POSITIONING:
[paste Section 1 from your Memory Index]

MEMORY — APPROVED SUBJECT LINES AND CTÁS:
[paste relevant entries from Section 2 of your Memory Index, if any]

Keep this context active for everything we produce in this session.
```

---

### Step 1 — Define the Email Brief

Before writing, declare exactly what this email is supposed to do:

```
I need to write [one email / a sequence of X emails] for [BRAND NAME].

Email type:
[ ] Cold outreach (first contact — they haven't opted in)
[ ] Nurture (they're on our list, in consideration phase)
[ ] Post-event (after a webinar, event, or lead magnet download)
[ ] Re-engagement (they've gone cold)
[ ] Sales / close (they've expressed interest, now we're asking)

Recipient:
[Specific person or segment — e.g., "a marketing director at a professional services firm who downloaded our guide last week"]

The one action this email must drive:
[e.g., "Book a 20-minute discovery call." / "Reply to this email." / "Click through to the case study."]

Revenue Gate:
[ ] A — This email should generate a response or booking within 7 days
[ ] B — This email should keep us present in the consideration window without pushing
[ ] C — This email should build brand familiarity over time

Context the reader needs (if any):
[e.g., "They attended our webinar on AI content systems last Tuesday." / "They're a new subscriber — no prior contact."]

Anything I must include or avoid:
[e.g., "Mention the case study about Ocean Jet." / "Do not mention pricing in the first email." / "Reference the event by name."]
```

---

### Step 2 — Subject Line First

The subject line determines whether the email gets read. Produce options before the body:

```
Before writing the email body, give me 5 subject line options for this email.

Requirements:
- No all-caps
- No excessive punctuation
- No "Quick question" or "Following up"
- Under 50 characters preferred
- Must reflect the email's actual content (no bait-and-switch)
- Match my voice: [paste your Voice Card formality and authority settings]

Also give me preview text for the 2 strongest options (50–80 characters).
```

Select one subject line before proceeding. The body must deliver on whatever the subject line promises.

---

### Step 3 — Write the Email Body

After selecting the subject line, write the body:

```
Write the body of this email using the subject line: "[selected subject line]"

Requirements:
- Open with the value or insight — not "I hope this email finds you well" or any variation of it
- Match Voice Card: pace [fast/moderate/slow], formality [setting + email modifier], authority [setting]
- Include one specific claim, example, or proof point — no abstract benefits
- One CTA only: "[your required action from Step 1]"
- Length: [under 150 words for cold outreach / 200–400 words for nurture or post-event]
- Do not use: [paste top 5 forbidden phrases from Rules Card]
```

---

### Step 4 — Run Brand Check

Once you have a draft you're considering:

```
Run a brand check on this email. Score it on:
D1 — Voice Alignment (check against my Voice Card, email platform modifier)
D2 — Forbidden Phrase Scan (check my Rules Card Layer 1)
D3 — Specificity (does it have at least one real example or data point?)
D5 — CTA Quality (one CTA, direct, not buried, not apologetic)
D6 — Email Rules (check my Rules Card Layer 5 for email)

Score out of 5. Verdict: Ship / Fix / Kill. Specific required changes if not Ship.
```

---

### Step 5 — If Writing a Sequence

For sequences of more than one email, plan the arc before writing individual emails:

```
Plan a [number]-email sequence for this scenario:
[brief description — e.g., "3-email post-webinar nurture sequence ending with a call CTA"]

For each email in the sequence, define:
1. Email number and send timing (e.g., Email 1 — sent immediately / Email 2 — 3 days later)
2. One-line description of what this email does
3. How it advances the reader toward the final CTA
4. What the subject line direction should be

Do not write the emails yet. Just the sequence plan.
```

Review and approve the sequence plan. Then write each email using Steps 2–4 above, in order.

---

### Step 6 — Final Check Before Sending

```
PRE-SEND CHECKLIST:
[ ] Subject line: no all-caps, no excessive punctuation, no "Quick question"?
[ ] Opening line: does it front-load value? (Not "Hope this finds you well.")
[ ] One specific example, client, or data point included?
[ ] One CTA — not zero, not two?
[ ] Unsubscribe mechanism included? (Required for all marketing emails)
[ ] Brand check: Ship verdict?
[ ] Read aloud: does it sound like a human who respects the reader's time?
```

---

### Step 7 — Log It

After sending:

```
[Date] — [Email subject line] — [Sequence or campaign name] — Brand check: [X/5] — Verdict: Ship — Send date: [date] — [Open rate / reply rate if measurable within 7 days]
```

If a subject line performs well (above 30% open rate for cold, above 40% for warm list) → add it to Memory Section 2 (Approved Copy Bank).

## Common Mistakes

**Opening with context.** "I'm reaching out because..." and "I came across your profile and..." are context. The reader doesn't need context — they need a reason to keep reading. Start with the interesting part.

**Two CTAs.** "Book a call or reply to this email" = no action. Pick one.

**Cold emails over 150 words.** Cold emails exist to earn a reply, not to explain everything. Under 150 words for first contact. Always.

**Weak subject lines on warm sequences.** Nurture email subject lines are often as bland as internal memos. They should earn the open even from someone who's already heard from you before.

**"Feel free to" CTAs.** "Feel free to book a call" signals that the ask might be an imposition. It's not. Own the ask.
