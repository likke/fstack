# Identity — Build Your Brand Voice Card
*Copy this entire prompt into ChatGPT or Claude. Then follow the instructions.*

---

You are a brand strategist helping me document my brand's voice, personality, and identity into a locked, reusable card. Every piece of content I produce will reference this card.

I'm going to give you writing samples from my brand. Your job is to extract what's actually true about my voice — not what I wish it was — and lock it into an 8-dimension Voice Card with real examples pulled directly from my content.

Do not invent a voice. Extract it from what I give you.

---

## STEP 1 — Give me your 3–5 best writing samples

Paste content you've written that already sounds right. Emails, LinkedIn posts, proposals, captions — anything you'd publish again. The more real and specific, the better.

If you don't have samples yet, write 3 sentences about the biggest problem your best client has right now. That's your starting point.

**Your samples:**
```
[PASTE YOUR WRITING SAMPLES HERE]
```

---

## STEP 2 — Build the Voice Card

After reading my samples, fill this table for me. For each dimension, give me a specific setting AND a real quote pulled directly from my samples.

| # | Dimension | Setting | What This Means | Real Example from My Content |
|---|-----------|---------|----------------|------------------------------|
| 1 | **PACE** | fast / moderate / slow | How long are my sentences? How fast do I get to the point? | [pull from samples] |
| 2 | **FORMALITY** | casual / professional / editorial | What room am I writing for — coffee chat, boardroom, or podcast? | [pull from samples] |
| 3 | **AUTHORITY** | peer / expert / mentor / commander | Do I assert or suggest? Teach or advise? | [pull from samples] |
| 4 | **HUMOR** | none / dry / warm / sharp | When and how humor shows up — if it does | [pull from samples] |
| 5 | **JARGON** | none / light / heavy | What do I assume the reader already knows? | [pull from samples] |
| 6 | **EMOTION** | restrained / controlled / open | How much feeling comes through? | [pull from samples] |
| 7 | **SPECIFICITY** | abstract / balanced / hyper-specific | Do I name names, numbers, tools — or speak in concepts? | [pull from samples] |
| 8 | **POV** | first / second / third | Do I write as I, you, or they by default? | [pull from samples] |

**Enforcement rule:** If AI generates content that violates 2 or more of these dimensions, the output is rejected and re-briefed — not edited.

---

## STEP 4 — Banned Phrases

Based on my samples, generate a banned phrases list — the words and constructions I never use, because they sound wrong for my brand.

Then add these defaults (every brand bans these):

```
BANNED PHRASES — [MY BRAND NAME]

From my voice (extracted from samples):
- [phrases that sound like someone else's brand]
- [hedging language that dilutes my confidence register]
- [jargon I'm not using]

Always banned (every brand):
- "Innovative solutions"
- "Leverage" / "Unlock" / "Empower"
- "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary"
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Take your X to the next level"
- "We're excited to announce..."
- "Seamless" / "Best-in-class" / "Synergy"
```

---

## STEP 5 — Platform Modifiers

My voice stays the same everywhere. My tone, pace, and CTA format shift per platform. Fill this in for the platforms I use most:

```
PLATFORM MODIFIERS:

LinkedIn:
- Formality: [adjustment — e.g., "+1 from default, more executive"]
- Pace: [e.g., "short hook, 3–5 body lines, hard close"]
- CTA: [e.g., "one low-pressure invitation — not 'DM me for pricing'"]
- Avoid: [e.g., "hashtag stacks, 'Thoughts?', self-congratulation"]

Email:
- Formality: [e.g., "match recipient's seniority"]
- Pace: [e.g., "front-load the value, long middle is earned"]
- CTA: [e.g., "one action per email, never buried"]
- Avoid: [e.g., "'Hope this finds you well', multiple asks"]

[Add other platforms as needed]
```

## OUTPUT FORMAT

Produce the completed Voice Card in this format:

```
VOICE CARD: [Brand Name]
Created: [date]
Status: LOCKED

DIMENSION TABLE:
[completed 8-row table with real examples]

SOUNDS LIKE / DOESN'T SOUND LIKE:
[5 contrast pairs]

BANNED PHRASES:
[complete list]

PLATFORM MODIFIERS:
[all active platforms]

VOICE TESTS (run every piece of content against these):
1. Best Client Test: Could my best client read this and say "that's exactly how they talk"?
2. Read-Aloud Test: Read it out loud. Does it sound like me at [MY TYPICAL SETTING]?
3. First-Line Test: Does line 1 earn line 2? If it's throat-clearing → cut it.
4. Cliché Scan: Does it contain a banned phrase? One strike = rewrite. Two = kill.
5. Specificity Test: Can any vague claim be replaced with a number, name, or date? If yes → rewrite.
```
