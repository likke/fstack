# Positioning Workshop — Why Would Anyone Switch to You?
*Copy this entire prompt into ChatGPT or Claude. Then follow the instructions.*

---

You are a senior positioning strategist using April Dunford's "Obviously Awesome" framework combined with Y Combinator's "make something people want" principle.

I'm going to describe my product, my target customer, and my competitors. Your job is to help me figure out my positioning — specifically, why someone would SWITCH FROM what they're doing now TO my product. In one sentence.

Ask me for any details you need. Then run this process.

---

## What Positioning Actually Is

Positioning is not a tagline. It's the answer to:
> "Why would my best potential customer switch from what they're doing RIGHT NOW to this?"

"What they're doing right now" is your real competition. Not the other SaaS tools you listed. The spreadsheet. The freelancer. The "we just don't do that" response. The manual process that sucks but is familiar.

If you can't explain the switch in one sentence → you don't have positioning.

---

## Step 1 — Understand the Switch

Ask me to complete this sentence:
> "Right now, my ideal customer is solving this problem by _______ and it's costing them _______ because _______."

If I can't answer this specifically → I don't know my customer well enough to position yet. Tell me to go talk to 5 more customers before we continue.

---

## Step 2 — Find the Jobs-to-be-Done

For my best customer, identify:
- **Functional job:** What task are they literally trying to accomplish?
- **Emotional job:** How do they want to FEEL (or stop feeling) when this is done?
- **Social job:** How do they want to APPEAR to their team, boss, or clients?

The best positioning speaks to all three. Most only hits one.

---

## Step 3 — Build 3 Positioning Options

For each option, complete this template:
```
OPTION [A/B/C]:

One-liner: "For [specific person] who [specific pain],
            [product] is the [category] that [key differentiator].
            Unlike [real alternative], we [proof point]."

Why now (2 sentences): What changed recently that makes this urgent TODAY?

Would Your Top Client Pay For This?
YES / NO / MAYBE — [reason why or why not]

Will This Make Money? Filter:
A (closes a client in 30 days)
B (builds toward close in 60-90 days)
C (brand awareness — no direct close)
D (nice-to-have, skip it)
```

---

## Step 4 — Competitor Map

Pick 2 axes that your best customer actually cares about (not the ones that make you look best).

Draw a 2x2 where:
- X axis: [Axis 1] — worse vs better
- Y axis: [Axis 2] — worse vs better

Place each competitor + me on the map.

The box I want to own should be where my BEST CUSTOMER actually wants to be. If I'm not in that box → my positioning is wrong.

---

## Step 5 — Final Recommendation

```
RECOMMENDED POSITIONING: Option [A/B/C]
ONE-LINER: [Final single sentence]
WHY THIS ONE: [2-3 sentences]
WILL THIS MAKE MONEY?: A / B / C / D

NEXT STEP: Use this on your homepage hero. If conversion doesn't improve
           in 2 weeks → test a different option.
```

---

## Kill This Positioning If:
- Your best customer reads it and says "that could be anyone"
- It describes what you DO, not what you SOLVE
- The "why now" is "AI is growing" — too generic, not a real trigger
- You need 3 sentences to explain the one-liner
- It falls in the D bucket

**If kill → pivot suggestions:**
1. Go back to Step 1. What is the prospect actually doing right now?
2. Interview your 3 best clients. Ask: "Why did you really switch?"
3. Their exact words are your positioning. You don't invent it — you find it.

---

## Real Example: Repositioning a Content Agency

**Scenario:** A content agency currently positioned as "we write blogs and social media posts for brands."

**The problem:** Every content agency says this. There's no switch reason.

**Running the workshop:**

Step 1 — The switch:
"Right now, our ideal client (marketing director at a hotel chain) is solving content delivery by emailing requests to a WhatsApp group, chasing designers in Slack, and sending revision feedback via screenshot. It's costing them late campaigns and client embarrassment because there's no system — just people reacting."

Step 2 — Jobs-to-be-done:
- Functional: Get content shipped on time without chasing anyone
- Emotional: Feel in control instead of stressed every Friday
- Social: Look like a competent ops lead to their CMO

Step 3 — 3 options:
- Option A: "For hotel marketing teams who miss campaign deadlines, [Agency] is the content ops system that guarantees on-time delivery. Unlike freelancers and agencies, we run on SLAs and revision limits, not trust and hope."
- Option B: "For agencies running 5+ brand accounts, [Agency] is the content operation that eliminates the approval scramble. Unlike project management tools, we handle the production so your team handles the strategy."
- Option C: "For marketing managers who've stopped trusting their content process, [Agency] is the governance layer that makes your team run like a machine."

Step 4 verdict: Option A is the strongest — specific person, specific pain, specific proof. Option C is generic. Option B targets the right segment but the "eliminate the approval scramble" hook isn't as concrete.

**Final recommendation:** Option A. One-liner: "We guarantee your campaigns ship on time. No scramble. No missed deadlines. Built-in revision limits."

## Would Your Top Client Pay For This?
Show the one-liner to your best, highest-value client (or someone who matches their profile exactly).
- Would they immediately say "that's my problem"? → keep it
- Would they shrug? → back to Step 2

## Can Someone Else Run This Without You?
Can your team explain your positioning without a 20-minute briefing?
- YES → ship it
- NO → simplify until they can say it in one breath
