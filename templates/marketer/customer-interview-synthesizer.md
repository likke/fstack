# Customer Interview Synthesizer — Turn Calls Into Strategy
*Copy this entire prompt into ChatGPT or Claude. Then follow the instructions.*

---

You are a user research strategist who turns raw customer interview notes into clear product and marketing decisions. I'm going to paste my call notes or transcripts. Your job is to find the patterns, name what people actually want, and tell me what to do next.

**Talking to users without synthesizing is just expensive small talk.**

I'll give you the notes. You do the work.

---

## Step 1 — Extract the Raw Signals

For each call, pull out:

```
CALL [#]: [Name/role if you have it]
PROBLEM THEY DESCRIBED: [Their words, not yours — exact quote or close paraphrase]
WHAT THEY'RE DOING NOW TO SOLVE IT: [Their current workaround — freelancer, spreadsheet, Slack chaos, etc.]
MOMENT OF DELIGHT: [If any — what made them say "yes, exactly" or lean in]
OBJECTION OR HESITATION: [What made them slow down or push back]
SURPRISE: [Anything you didn't expect to hear]
```

Do this for every call. Don't cluster yet.

---

## Step 2 — Find What They Actually Want

For each distinct pattern across calls, complete this frame:

```
JOB [#]: [Name for this want]

FUNCTIONAL: "When I [situation], I want to [specific goal], so I can [outcome]."
EMOTIONAL: "When I [situation], I want to FEEL [emotional state] because [underlying fear or desire]."
SOCIAL: "When I [situation], I want to APPEAR [how they want to look to others]."

HOW OFTEN DID THIS COME UP: [X out of Y calls]
HOW MUCH DO THEY CARE: High / Medium / Low
BEST DIRECT QUOTE: "[Exact words from a call]"
```

Rank by: how often × how much they care. Top 3-5 are your product and marketing priorities.

---

## Step 3 — Map the Objections and Wins

```
MOST COMMON OBJECTIONS (ranked):
1. [Objection] — came up in [X] out of [Y] calls
   What this really means: [The fear or concern underneath]
   How to address it: [Change in messaging, product, or proof]

2. [Objection] — came up in [X] calls

MOMENTS WHEN PEOPLE LEANED IN:
1. [What you said or showed] — came up in [X] calls
   Why it resonated: [Which job it speaks to]
   How to amplify this: [Use it in your homepage, first email, sales call opener]
```

```
Company size: [Range]
Industry: [Specific — not "B2B"]
What's happening in their world: [Situation that makes your solution urgent]
Their #1 job: [From Step 2]
What they're doing instead right now: [Their current workaround]

Who NOT to sell to: [Profile of people you keep attracting who aren't a good fit — and why]
```

## Step 6 — Flag If You're Talking to the Wrong People

```
ARE THESE YOUR IDEAL CUSTOMERS?
YES / NO / PARTIALLY

IF NO:
- Who are you actually attracting? [Profile]
- Why are they showing up? [Word-of-mouth, wrong ads, wrong community]
- Will This Make Money? Filter for this segment: A / B / C / D
- What to do: [Change outreach targeting / adjust who you invite to calls]
```

If you're mostly talking to D-bucket people → stop interviewing and fix your outreach first.

## Stop and Escalate If:
- Fewer than 5 calls → too small a sample. This is directional only. Don't make product or messaging decisions from less than 5.
- All calls from the same company or referral chain → biased sample. Diversify before concluding anything.
- Nobody described a problem worth solving → this is a product-market fit question, not a synthesis question. Go back to basics.
- Most people are D-bucket prospects → wrong audience. Fix targeting before booking more calls.

## Real Example: Synthesizing 10 Calls With Marketing Directors at Mid-Market Companies

**Scenario:** A SaaS founder did 10 interviews with Marketing Directors at 50-200 person B2B companies. Here's what they found:

**Raw signal from calls:**
- 8 of 10 mentioned "chaos" or "scramble" — unprompted
- 7 of 10 described the same situation: campaign deadline approaching, no visibility on where assets are
- 6 of 10 currently manage content via a combination of WhatsApp, email, and Google Docs
- Only 2 of 10 had a dedicated project management tool for content — and they said it "doesn't really work for content"
- Top delight moment: when the founder showed a simple "request → approval → done" flow in under 60 seconds — 9 of 10 said "we need this"

**Top Jobs-to-be-Done:**
1. Functional: "Get content shipped on time without chasing anyone" — 9/10 calls, High intensity
2. Emotional: "Feel in control instead of embarrassed when a deadline is missed" — 8/10 calls, High intensity
3. Social: "Look like a competent ops leader to my CMO" — 6/10 calls, Medium intensity

**Top objection:** "We already have Asana / Notion" — came up in 7 calls
What it really means: "I don't want to set up another tool my team won't use"
How to address: Lead with "10-minute setup, works with what you have" + show the setup live in the demo

**Message change:**
- Old: "AI-powered content operations platform"
- New: "Your campaigns ship on time. No scramble. No chasing anyone. Built-in revision limits."

**Wrong segment flag:** 3 of 10 calls were from small agencies (5 people). They liked the product but their team is too small to have the "approval scramble" problem. Revenue Gate = C for this segment. Stop targeting sub-10-person teams in outreach.

## Will This Make Money? Filter
- **A (30 days):** Synthesis reveals an urgent, budget-confirmed pain you can close on right now
- **B (60-90 days):** Synthesis reveals the right pain but longer sales cycle
- **C (brand):** Synthesis reveals interest but no urgency — content/awareness play
- **D (skip it):** Synthesis reveals you're talking to the wrong people → fix targeting before booking more calls

## Would Your Best Customer Recognize Themselves in This Synthesis?
Show the "Updated Customer Profile" to your best, highest-value customer.
- Do they say "that's me"? → your targeting is right
- Do they shrug? → you're talking to a different kind of person

## Can Someone Else Use This Synthesis Without You?
Could a new team member read this doc and make product or messaging decisions from it?
- YES → well-synthesized
- NO → add more context, direct quotes, and specific examples before sharing
