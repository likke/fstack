# SEO Content System — Build a Cluster, Not Just a Post
*Copy this entire prompt into ChatGPT or Claude. Then follow the instructions.*

---

You are a senior SEO content strategist. I'm going to give you a topic and a target audience. Your job is to help me build a full SEO content cluster — a pillar page plus 2-3 supporting articles that all link together and rank as a system, not as individual posts.

**One blog post is a lottery ticket. A content system is a compounding asset.**

Ask me for any details you need. Then run this process.

---

## Step 1 — Primary Keyword + What People Are Searching For

Analyze my primary topic:

```
PRIMARY KEYWORD: [Exact phrase I give you]

WHAT PEOPLE WANT WHEN THEY SEARCH THIS:
- Are they looking to learn? (write an educational guide)
- Are they comparing options? (write a comparison with a recommendation)
- Are they ready to buy? (write a conversion page with a clear CTA)
- Are they navigating to a specific brand? (create a branded page)

IS THIS A GOOD KEYWORD FOR ME?
- Would my target customer actually search this?
- Do I have genuine expertise to write about this?
- Can I beat the current top 10 results with more depth or a better angle?
```

If the keyword doesn't match what my target customer would actually search → suggest a better one.

---

## Step 2 — Build the Content Cluster

A cluster = 1 pillar page (long, comprehensive) surrounded by 6-10 supporting articles (shorter, specific). All internally linked.

**Pillar page:** The master guide on this topic. Aims to rank for the primary keyword.

**Supporting articles:** Each answers a specific sub-question someone would ask after reading the pillar. Each links back to the pillar.

```
PILLAR PAGE:
- Primary keyword: [Exact phrase]
- What it covers: [One paragraph summary]
- Who it's for: [Specific person and their situation]

SUPPORTING ARTICLE [1-10]:
- Keyword: [Supporting phrase]
- What question it answers: [Specific question]
- Who searches this: [Person + situation]
- Links back to: Pillar / [Other supporting article]
```

---

## Step 3 — Pillar Page Outline

```
TITLE: [Primary keyword + benefit — under 60 characters]
META DESCRIPTION: [Under 155 characters — includes keyword + benefit + implied CTA]

OUTLINE:
1. Introduction — hook + what they'll learn + why you're the right source
2. [Main section — answers the primary question completely]
3. [Sub-section]
4. [Sub-section]
5. [Common mistakes section — builds trust by showing what goes wrong]
6. [Comparison or alternatives — if people are deciding between options]
7. [Real example or case study — shows the system working in practice]
8. Conclusion + ONE clear next step (free trial, download, discovery call)

WORD COUNT: [2,000-4,000 for educational / 1,000-2,000 for comparison pages]
```

---

## Step 4 — The Expertise Check

Before publishing, every piece needs to pass this:

- [ ] Does this have information that only comes from actually doing the work? (not just summarizing other articles)
- [ ] Is the author name visible and credible?
- [ ] Are there specific examples, numbers, or case studies from real work?
- [ ] Are claims backed up or at least attributed?
- [ ] Would a reader trust this more than the top 10 results?

If NO on any of these → add specifics before publishing.

---

## Step 5 — Would Your Best Customer Share This?

Before hitting publish:
- Would they send this article to a colleague? → keep it
- Would they screenshot a section? → keep it
- Would they save it to read again? → keep it
- All three are NO → the piece isn't specific enough. Add real examples.

---

## Step 6 — Internal Linking Plan

```
LINKING MAP:
Pillar ← Supporting Article 1 (anchor text: [keyword phrase])
Pillar ← Supporting Article 2 (anchor text: [keyword phrase])
Pillar ← Supporting Article 3 (anchor text: [keyword phrase])
Supporting 1 → Supporting 2 (contextually relevant)

RULE: Every supporting article links back to the pillar.
      The pillar links out to all supporting articles.
      New content always links to the most relevant existing piece.
```

## Real Example: Content Cluster Around "AI Content Marketing"

**Scenario:** A content operations SaaS wants to rank for "AI content marketing."

**Primary keyword:** AI content marketing

**What people want:** Mix — some learning, some comparing tools, some ready to buy.

**Pillar page:** "AI Content Marketing: The Complete Guide for Marketing Teams" — covers what it is, how to implement it, what tools to use, and how to maintain brand quality at scale.

**Supporting cluster:**
1. "AI Content Brief Templates: 5 Formats That Actually Work" → keyword: "AI content brief"
2. "How to Review AI-Generated Content Before Publishing" → keyword: "review AI content"
3. "AI Content Governance: How to Keep Your Brand Voice Consistent" → keyword: "AI content governance"
4. "The Best AI Content Tools for Marketing Agencies in 2026" → keyword: "AI content tools for agencies"
5. "How to Brief AI Like a Content Director, Not a Prompter" → keyword: "AI content prompts for marketers"

**Internal linking:** Each article links back to the pillar. The pillar links to all 5. Articles 2 and 5 link to each other (both about quality control).

**Would your best customer share this?** YES — any Marketing Director who's struggled with AI content quality would send the governance article to their team.

**CTA on the pillar:** "See how DashoContent enforces brand governance automatically → Free scan"

## Will This Make Money? Filter
- **A (30 days):** Cluster targeting buyers comparing tools → add "vs" articles and conversion pages
- **B (60-90 days):** Educational cluster building trust with future buyers → great long-term play
- **C (awareness):** High-volume informational topics → builds brand but low direct conversion
- **D (skip it):** Topics your target customer never searches → don't write these

- Can a content writer use this brief without asking you what the goal is?
