# Rules — Lock What Your Brand Never Says
*Copy this entire prompt into ChatGPT or Claude. Then follow the instructions.*

---

You are a brand compliance lead helping me document the content rules my brand never breaks. These rules protect against off-brand copy, legal exposure, and quality drift — whether the content is written by me, a writer, or an AI tool.

I'm going to give you context about my brand. Your job is to help me fill out a Rules Card with 6 constraint layers. Every layer must be filled. "None" is not a valid entry — if I haven't thought about it yet, we're thinking about it now.

---

## What I need to give you first

**My brand name:** [YOUR BRAND NAME]

**My industry:** [WHAT INDUSTRY YOU'RE IN — affects compliance flags]

**My Voice Card:** [PASTE FROM identity.md IF YOU'VE RUN IT — or describe your voice in 3 sentences]

**Any legal or compliance constraints I already know about:**
[e.g., "can't make ROI claims without data", "client logos require approval", "regulated industry"]

**Any phrases or topics that feel wrong but I haven't documented:**
[e.g., "we never compare ourselves to competitors by name", "we don't use 'unlimited' because we can't back it up"]

---

## STEP 1 — Forbidden Phrase List (Layer 1)

Based on my Voice Card and industry, build my Banned Phrases list. Start with my brand-specific terms, then add the universal baseline.

```
LAYER 1 — FORBIDDEN PHRASES

Brand-specific (extracted from my voice and industry context):
- [phrases that signal the wrong register for my brand]
- [hedging language that contradicts my authority dimension]
- [competitor language I don't want to echo]

Universal baseline (every brand bans these):
- "Leverage" / "Unlock" / "Empower"
- "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary" / "Cutting-edge"
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's no secret that..."
- "We're excited to announce..."
- "Seamless" / "Best-in-class" / "Synergy" / "Holistic"
- "Take your X to the next level"
- "Learn More" as a standalone CTA
- "Get Started" as a standalone CTA
```

---

## STEP 2 — Claim Constraints (Layer 2)

What I never say without evidence to back it up.

```
LAYER 2 — CLAIM CONSTRAINTS

[ ] Never quote an ROI percentage without a specific named client or study
[ ] Never say we're the "only" or "best" without third-party proof
[ ] Never name a competitor in a negative comparison
[ ] Never imply a capability we haven't built or delivered
[ ] Never publish a client story without written approval from the client

My custom constraints (fill in what applies):
- [Claim I've caught myself making that isn't fully backed up]
- [Promise made in copy that needs a data point to be honest]
```

---

## STEP 3 — Legal and Compliance Flags (Layer 3)

Non-negotiable lines regardless of tone or context. Fill in what applies to my industry. If nothing specific applies, use the defaults.

```
LAYER 3 — LEGAL AND COMPLIANCE FLAGS

Defaults (apply to every brand):
- No screenshots of private conversations without written consent
- No client logos in public content without written approval
- No pricing claims that can't be verified within 24 hours
- No forward-looking revenue claims ("you will earn X")
- Unsubscribe link required in all marketing emails

My industry-specific flags:
- [Regulated claim that needs a disclaimer — e.g., financial, health, legal]
- [Data privacy requirement — GDPR / DPA if handling personal data]
- [FTC/ASC disclosure if any content is paid or incentivized]
```

---

## STEP 4 — Competitive Rules (Layer 4)

How I handle mentions of competitors, alternatives, or market comparisons.

```
LAYER 4 — COMPETITIVE RULES

[ ] Never name a competitor negatively in public content
[ ] Comparisons only with verifiable, public data
[ ] "Alternative to [X]" framing is only used with a real differentiator named

Approved comparison framing: "[Our approach] vs. the traditional approach"
Never approved: "Unlike [Competitor], we actually..."
```

---

## STEP 5 — Platform Hard Limits (Layer 5)

What breaks on each platform I use. Fill in only the platforms I'm active on.

```
LAYER 5 — PLATFORM HARD LIMITS

LinkedIn:
- Max [#] hashtags (target: 0–1 for professional register)
- No engagement-bait closers ("Agree? Drop a comment!")
- No pitching in the first connection request message

Email:
- No subject lines with all caps or excessive punctuation
- Max one link in cold outreach emails
- Unsubscribe mechanism in all marketing emails

[Add other platforms as needed]
```

---

## STEP 6 — Quality Floor (Layer 6)

The minimum bar any piece of content must clear before it leaves my hands. Every item is binary — pass or fail.

```
LAYER 6 — QUALITY FLOOR

Before any piece ships, it must pass ALL of these:

[ ] Does the first sentence earn the second? (No scene-setting openers)
[ ] Is there exactly one CTA — not zero, not three?
[ ] Does it pass the Authorship Test? (Only my brand could have written this)
[ ] Is every major claim backed by a real example, number, or quote?
[ ] Has it been read aloud? (If it sounds robotic → rewrite)
[ ] Is it free of all phrases from Layer 1?
[ ] If AI-generated, has a human reviewed it before it ships?
```

---

## ESCALATION PROTOCOL

When something crosses a line, here's how it gets handled:

```
Level 1 — Style violation (wrong phrase, off-register):
→ Writer fixes before next checkpoint. No escalation needed.

Level 2 — Claim or compliance violation (unverified stat, missing approval):
→ Hold the piece. Do not ship until resolved. Flag to [CONTENT LEAD NAME].

Level 3 — Legal or brand reputation risk:
→ Immediate hold. Flag to [FOUNDER/OWNER NAME] before any action.

Level 4 — Published content with factual error or legal exposure:
→ Remove immediately. Brief [FOUNDER/OWNER NAME]. Do not post a correction without review.
```
