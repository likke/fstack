BANNED PHRASES — DashoContent

From our voice:
- "Content is king"
- "Storytelling" used as a standalone strategy
- "Brand awareness" without a metric attached
- "Authentic" (show it, don't claim it)
- "Thought leadership" (describe the thought, skip the label)
- "We help brands find their voice" (we build systems, not voices)
- "At scale" without specifying what scale means
- Any opening that starts with "In today's..."
- "Feel free to reach out" as a CTA closer
- "Hope this finds you well" as an email opener

Always banned (every brand):
- "Innovative solutions"
- "Leverage" / "Unlock" / "Empower"
- "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary"
- "Seamless" / "Best-in-class" / "Synergy"
- "Take your X to the next level"
- "We're excited to announce..."
- "It's no secret that..."


PLATFORM MODIFIERS:

LinkedIn (primary channel):
- Formality: +0.5 (slightly more executive than default — we're speaking to decision-makers)
- Pace: Faster. Hook on line 1. Body in 3–5 punchy lines. No long-form narrative unless sharing a framework.
- CTA: One low-pressure invitation. "If this sounds like your team, reply and tell me where it breaks." — Never "DM me for pricing."
- Avoid: Hashtag stacks (max 1), "Thoughts?", "Agree?", self-congratulation, carousel-bait openers

Email (outbound and nurture):
- Formality: Match recipient's seniority. Marketing manager = professional-direct. CMO = tighter, more peer-level.
- Pace: Front-load value. First sentence must earn the second. Cold emails under 150 words.
- CTA: One action per email. Never buried. "Book 20 minutes here: [link]" — not "feel free to reach out if you'd like to discuss further"
- Avoid: "Hope this finds you well", "I wanted to reach out", "Just following up", multiple asks

Client Deliverables (reports, briefs, audits):
- Formality: Professional + precise. We are the expert in this document.
- Pace: Structured. Headers earn their place. Every section has a finding and a recommendation.
- CTA: Clear next step at the end of every deliverable. Not "let us know if you have questions."
- Avoid: Vague conclusions, hedged recommendations, passive voice for our own point of view

Proposals and Pitches:
- Formality: High. This is a business document.
- Pace: Front-load the diagnosis. If we understand their problem better than they do, say so in paragraph one.
- CTA: One ask, clearly stated. "Sign here to begin" or "Reply to confirm the start date."
- Avoid: Long agency credentials sections before the problem statement, feature lists without outcomes, "we believe" for facts we know


VOICE TESTS (run every piece against these):
1. Best Client Test: Could Aboitiz Land, Ocean Jet, or our most demanding client read this and say "that's exactly how DashoContent talks"?
2. Read-Aloud Test: Read it out loud at a pace a confident professional would use. Does it sound human?
3. First-Line Test: Does line 1 earn line 2? If it's context-setting → cut it.
4. Cliché Scan: Does it contain a banned phrase? One strike = rewrite. Two = kill.
5. Specificity Test: Can any vague claim be replaced with a number, name, or date? If yes → rewrite.
