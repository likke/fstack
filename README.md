# founderstack

**A founder's operating system for content ops companies — built on the same principles as Garry Tan's gstack, adapted for solo founders running agencies, SaaS, or hybrid models.**

Inspired by [gstack](https://github.com/garrytan/gstack). Adapted for founders who are:
- Running a content ops or marketing services business
- Working with a small team (1-5 people)
- Trying to get to $5k-$10k MRR with limited runway
- The sole decision-maker across product, sales, and operations

MIT License. Fork it. Make it yours.

## The Core Idea

YC companies move faster than their competitors not because they work harder — but because they **make fewer wrong decisions.**

gstack gives engineers a virtual engineering team. founderstack gives **founders** a virtual operating team:

| Role | Skill | What it catches |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Advisor | `/office-hours` | Wrong problem, wrong framing |
| CEO | `/ceo-review` | Wrong scope, wrong product decision |
| CTO | `/eng-review` | Wrong architecture, hidden technical debt |
| UX Lead | `/ux-review` | Wrong user experience, client friction |
| Release Manager | `/ship` | Wrong deployment, client-breaking changes |
| COO | `/retro` | Wrong week — what slipped and why |
| GTM Lead | `/gtm-review` | Wrong campaign, wrong message, wrong target |

## The Three Core Principles

### 1. The Client Test
Before any feature, offer, or campaign:
> **"Would your best client pay for this outcome in their first 7 days?"**

If yes → build/sell it.
If no → kill it or defer it until you have more signal.

Your best client is your proof of concept. Everything else is a hypothesis.

### 2. The Maintainability Check
Before any code ships:
> **"Can your smallest team member maintain this alone in 6 months without documentation?"**

If yes → ship it.
If no → simplify it first.

Bus factor = 1 on most small teams. Design for that reality.

### 3. The Revenue Gate
Every task, feature, or campaign gets bucketed:
- **A: Closes a client within 30 days**
- **B: Retains an existing client**
- **C: Reduces burn**
- **D: Everything else**

D doesn't get worked on. Not until you hit your revenue target.

## The Skills

### `/office-hours` — Challenge the idea before you build it
**When:** Any time you have an idea for a new feature, offer, or pivot.
**What it does:** Extracts the real pain underneath your idea, challenges 3 premises, generates 3 approaches (smallest test, best client path, scalable path), recommends the narrowest wedge.

### `/ceo-review` — Is this the right thing to build?
**When:** Before any sprint starts or any feature gets assigned.
**What it does:** Pressure-tests scope, applies the Client Test and Revenue Gate, recommends ship/simplify/defer/kill.

### `/eng-review` — Is this safe to ship?
**When:** Before any PR merges.
**What it does:** Checks maintainability, client impact, security flags, technical debt, and test coverage.

### `/ux-review` — Would a real client use this?
**When:** Before any feature goes live to clients.
**What it does:** Simulates a first-time, mildly-stressed client using the feature. Catches friction, AI slop, and confusing flows.

### `/ship` — Make every deployment safe and documented
**When:** Every deployment to production.
**What it does:** Pre-ship smoke test checklist, rollback plan, client impact assessment, post-ship monitoring.

### `/retro` — Make invisible work visible
**When:** Every Friday or Monday morning.
**What it does:** Surfaces what shipped, what slipped, delivery health, CEO decisions, and the single most important thing for next week.

### `/gtm-review` — Is this the right campaign?
**When:** Before any outreach, campaign, or new channel goes live.
**What it does:** Applies the Client Test and Revenue Gate to GTM decisions. Flags wrong message, wrong target, wrong channel.

## The Sprint Rhythm

| When | What |
|------|------|
| Monday 8AM | Weekly CEO brief — priorities, health check |
| Monday (L10 time) | Team sync — scorecard, issues, rocks |
| Monday post-sync | Hand team their sprint tasks |
| Friday | Quick async retro — did we ship? any blockers? |

## How to Adapt This to Your Business

1. **Replace "best client" with your actual best client profile** — the one with the highest margin, lowest revision count, and happiest relationship. That's your ICP template.

2. **Set your revenue target** — the number that makes you cash-flow positive. Every D-bucket task gets killed until you hit it.

3. **Define your one offer** — the outcome you sell, not the feature. Strip all feature language from your external-facing materials.

4. **Install the sprint rhythm** — if you don't have a fixed weekly meeting, nothing will catch the drift until it's a crisis.
