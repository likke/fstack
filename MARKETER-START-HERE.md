## The 8 Skills That Matter to You

### 1. Brief Review — AI Content Quality Gate
Review your brief BEFORE you produce content. Review AI output BEFORE you publish it.
Catches: vague briefs, generic AI copy, off-brand language, missing CTAs.
→ [templates/marketer/brief-review.md](templates/marketer/brief-review.md)

### 2. GTM Review — Is This Campaign Worth Running?
Pressure-test any campaign, ad, or outreach sequence before you spend time or money.
Catches: wrong audience, feature-first copy, no kill criterion, wasted budget.
→ [templates/marketer/gtm-review.md](templates/marketer/gtm-review.md)

### 3. Positioning Workshop — Why Would Anyone Switch to You?
Build clear, specific positioning using April Dunford's JTBD framework.
Catches: "we do everything" messaging, no clear switch reason, ignoring the real competition.
→ [templates/marketer/positioning-workshop.md](templates/marketer/positioning-workshop.md)

### 4. Experiment Designer — Test It Before You Scale It
Design a rigorous experiment with a kill criterion before anything launches.
Catches: vague hypotheses, no success criteria, scaling campaigns that don't work.
→ [templates/marketer/experiment-designer.md](templates/marketer/experiment-designer.md)

### 5. SEO Content System — Build a Cluster, Not Just a Post
Turn any topic into a pillar page + supporting articles that rank as a system.
Catches: one-off posts with no links, keyword targeting that misses your audience, thin content.
→ [templates/marketer/seo-content-system.md](templates/marketer/seo-content-system.md)

### 6. Launch Orchestrator — Plan the Launch Before Launch Day
Build a day-by-day launch sequence with copy, seeding list, and asset checklist.
Catches: uncoordinated launches, weak hooks, no community seeding, improvising on launch day.
→ [templates/marketer/launch-orchestrator.md](templates/marketer/launch-orchestrator.md)

### 7. Cohort Review — Are We Profitable Yet?
Analyze which customers are worth keeping and which channels are losing you money.
Catches: vanity metrics, unprofitable acquisition channels, churn you haven't noticed yet.
→ [templates/marketer/cohort-review.md](templates/marketer/cohort-review.md)

### 8. Customer Interview Synthesizer — Turn Calls Into Strategy
Distill raw interview notes into Jobs-to-be-Done, objections, and updated positioning.
Catches: talking to the wrong people, building for imaginary customers, missing the real pain.
→ [templates/marketer/customer-interview-synthesizer.md](templates/marketer/customer-interview-synthesizer.md)

## Start Here: The Content Loop

If you only use one thing from fstack, use this.

The Content Loop is the system for using AI to create content without losing your brand voice:

```
Load brand context (context-setup)
       ↓
Validate the idea (office-hours)
       ↓
Review the brief (brief-review MODE 1)
       ↓
Produce (AI writes the draft)
       ↓
Review the output (brief-review MODE 2)
       ↓
Score against your standards (brand-check)
       ↓
Pressure-test the campaign (gtm-review)
       ↓
Publish
```

Most marketers skip straight to "Produce" and wonder why their AI content sounds generic. The loop — and the brand identity layer that powers it — is what fixes that.

Full walkthrough with real examples: [templates/marketer/content-loop.md](templates/marketer/content-loop.md)

## Three Rules That Apply to Every Skill

**1. If it could have been written by anyone, rewrite it.**
Generic AI output is invisible. Your job is to make AI write like you, not like the average of everything it was trained on.

**2. Every campaign needs a kill criterion.**
Before any campaign launches, write: "We will stop this if [metric] doesn't reach [number] in [days]." If you can't write that sentence, don't launch.

**3. Your best, highest-value client is your filter.**
For every piece of content, every campaign, every positioning option — ask: would my best client pay for this outcome? Would they share this? If not, fix it first.
